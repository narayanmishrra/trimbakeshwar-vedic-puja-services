What is Narayan Nagbali Puja? (Understanding Narayan Bali vs Nagbali)

Narayan Nagbali is a vital multi-day ritual composed of two distinct ceremonies: Narayan Bali, which helps pacify ancestral curses and unsatisfied souls (Pitru Dosh), and Nagbali, which absolves the sin of killing a snake. Together, they bring profound peace and prosperity to the family lineage.

Why is Narayan Nagbali Performed Only at Trimbakeshwar Temple?

This powerful ritual is exclusively performed at the Jyotirlinga Temple in Trimbakeshwar. The holy presence of the Godavari River and the spiritual authority of the local Purohit Sangh make this location uniquely suited for such profound Ancient Vedic Vidhi. An Authorized Panditji ensures that the strict protocols laid out in the Garuda Purana are meticulously followed.

3-Day Detailed Narayan Nagbali Procedure

The ceremony spans three days, each with specific rites dedicated to different aspects of dosh nivaran.

Day 1: Sankalp, Ganapati Pujan & Wheat Flour Pind Daan

The ritual begins with a formal vow (Sankalp) led by a Tamrapatradhari Guruji. Devotees participate in Ganapati Pujan and offer Pind Daan using wheat flour, invoking the blessings of Lord Vishnu to calm restless ancestral spirits.

Day 2: Nagbali Puja & Pitru Dosh Shanti Mantras

The second day focuses on the Nagbali Puja. Offerings are made to a specially prepared wheat flour snake to seek forgiveness for any harm caused to serpents. Powerful Pitru Dosh Shanti Mantras are chanted to elevate the ancestral souls.

Day 3: Tarpan at Kushavarta Kund & Final Offerings

The concluding day involves Tarpan (water oblations) at the sacred Kushavarta Kund. Final offerings, charitable donations to the Brahmins, and prayers for the family's future well-being mark the completion of the 3-day vidhi.
