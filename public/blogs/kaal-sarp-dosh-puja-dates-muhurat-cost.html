What is Kaal Sarp Dosh and Why Perform it at Trimbakeshwar?

Kaal Sarp Dosh occurs when all planets align between Rahu and Ketu in one's birth chart. Trimbakeshwar is the supreme site for this ritual, nestled near the sacred Godavari River and home to the divine Jyotirlinga Temple. Performing this puja here with an Authorized Panditji using Ancient Vedic Vidhi ensures complete dosh nivaran.

Symptoms of Kaal Sarp Yoga in Your Kundali

Common symptoms include constant delays in career, financial instability, marriage obstacles, and vivid dreams involving snakes. If you experience these, consulting a Purohit Sangh registered pandit is highly recommended.

12 Types of Kaal Sarp Dosh (Anant, Kulik, Vasuki, etc.)

Based on planetary positions, there are 12 primary types: Anant, Kulik, Vasuki, Shankhapal, Padma, Mahapadma, Takshak, Karkotak, Shankhachur, Ghatak, Vishdhar, and Sheshnag. Each type requires specific Ancient Vedic Vidhi for proper resolution.

Kaal Sarp Puja Procedure & Duration

The entire ritual is a complete 1-Day process designed to neutralize the negative planetary alignments.

Ritual Timeline: From Kushavarta Kund Dip to Temple Sankalp

The procedure begins with a purifying holy dip in the Kushavarta Kund. Following this, the devotee takes a formal Sankalp (vow) at the puja sthal near the Jyotirlinga Temple. The Authorized Panditji then leads the Ganapati Pujan, Rahu-Ketu Jaap, and silver snake offerings over approximately 2 to 3 hours.
