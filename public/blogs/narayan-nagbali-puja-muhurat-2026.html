Introduction to Narayan Nagbali Puja

Narayan Nagbali is an extremely critical Vedic ritual performed over three days at Trimbakeshwar to appease ancestral spirits and eliminate Pitru Dosh. Given the importance of the ritual, executing it on shastras-recommended dates and auspicious muhurats ensures the best outcomes for descendants.

Adhik Maas Restrictive Period in 2026

Devotees must note that due to the occurrence of Adhik Maas (extra lunar month) in 2026, all auspicious activities and Vedic rituals like Narayan Nagbali are strictly prohibited from May 16th to June 14th. There are no available muhurat dates during this month.

3-Day Ritual Flow

Day 1: Bathing at Kushavarta Kund, taking the holy Sankalp, and starting the Narayan Bali rituals with wheat flour pind offerings.

Day 2: Nagbali rituals seeking forgiveness for past actions towards serpents, followed by specialized Havans.

Day 3: Final Tarpan, offering donations and clothes to Brahmins, and seeking final ancestral blessings.
